Interactive Visualization of the Google Books Dataset

Welcome to our interactive visualization of the Google Books dataset. This dashboard provides insights into various aspects of the dataset, including trends over time, genre popularity, and more. Below, you will find different charts showcasing:

1. Books Published Over Time - Scatterplot illustrating the number of books published over the years, categorized by language.

2. Genre/Author Popularity - Bar chart showing the popularity of the top genres and authors.

3. Book Popularity Bubble Chart - A bubble chart displaying book popularity based on various metrics.

4. Word Count in Titles - Analysis of the word count in book titles.

5. Zoomable Sunburst Chart - An interactive sunburst chart exploring the hierarchical structure of the dataset.

This visualization is designed to offer an in-depth perspective on the dataset, allowing you to delve into and grasp different facets of book publishing trends. Each visualization is crafted to be intuitive and engaging, featuring interactive elements like sliders, tooltips, and annotations to enrich your exploration of the data.

Explore the dataset by navigating through the sections and use the provided controls to delve deeper into the data. We hope you find these visualizations insightful and engaging.

Books Published Over Time

This scatter plot illustrates the distribution of books published over time, categorized by language. Each point represents a book, with its position determined by the publication year and page count. The color of each point corresponds to the book's language, allowing for a visual comparison of trends across different languages.

Interactions:

Tooltips: Hover over points to view detailed information about each book, including its title, author(s), genre(s), language, publication year, and much more.

Annotations: Annotations highlight significant trends and patterns, such as peaks in publication over specific years or prominent languages during certain periods.

Year Slider: Use the slider to filter books by their publication year, enabling you to focus on specific time periods and observe trends over time.

Legends: The color legend helps identify the language categories of the books, making it easy to analyze language-specific trends and distributions.

Highlighting: Points will change color on hover to distinguish between different languages, helping you to visually compare data points more easily.

Genre/Author Popularity

This bar chart visualizes the top 10 genres or authors based on the number of books. Each bar represents a genre or author, and its length corresponds to the number of books in that category.

Interactions:

Tooltips: Hover over bars to view detailed information about each genre or author, including the exact number of books.

Annotations: Annotations highlight significant trends and notable observations, such as the most popular genre or author.

Dropdown Menu: Select between genres and authors to switch the data displayed in the chart.

Sorting: Use radio buttons to sort the bars in ascending or descending order based on the number of books.

Highlighting: Bars will change color on hover to emphasize the selected genre or author, making it easier to identify and compare them.

Book Popularity Bubble Chart

This bubble chart visualizes books based on their average rating and the number of pages. Each bubble represents a book, with its position determined by the average rating and the number of pages, while the size of the bubble reflects the book's popularity, measured by the number of voters.

Interactions:

Tooltips: Hover over bubbles to see detailed information about each book, including its title, author(s), maturity rating, average rating, publication year and the number of pages.

Annotations: Annotations highlight key observations and trends in the chart, such as clusters of books with similar ratings or page counts, and notable popular books.

Legends: Click on color legends to highlight bubbles from specific rating ranges or page counts, making it easier to analyze the data.

Highlighting: Bubbles will change to a distinct color on hover, allowing you to focus on individual books and compare their average ratings, page counts, and popularity.

Word Count in Titles

This chart analyzes the frequency of word counts in book titles. Each block in the tree map represents a unique word count, with its size indicating how common that word count is across the dataset. Discover how often different word counts appear and identify trends in title lengths.

Interactions:

Tooltips: Hover over blocks to view detailed information about the word counts and their frequencies.

Annotations: Explore the visualization to uncover patterns and trends in word counts and understand which title lengths are most prevalent.

Zoomable Sunburst Chart

This interactive sunburst chart provides a hierarchical view of the book data, organized by language, category, author, publisher, and publication year. Each segment represents a part of the hierarchy, with its size indicating the number of books associated with that segment. Dive into different levels to explore detailed relationships and understand how various attributes contribute to the overall dataset.

Interactions:

Tooltips: Hover over segments to see detailed information about the books in that segment, including their names and values.

Zoom: Click on segments to zoom into specific parts of the hierarchy and explore different levels in more detail, and click in the center of the circle to zoom out and return to previous levels. Use zoom to get a closer look at the data.

Annotations: Explore the visualization to uncover key insights and patterns in book data across different categories, languages, and authors.
